COMPLEMENTARY CONVENTION TO THE DECLARATION OF SOVEREIGNTY OVER THE 200-MILE MARITIME ZONE Lima, Peru, December 4, 1954 CONSIDERING: That Chile, Ecuador and Peru have proclaimed their sovereignty over the sea that bathes the coasts of their respective countries, up to a minimum distance of two hundred miles marine from the aforementioned coasts, including the soil and subsoil that to that Zone Maritime correspond; That the Governments of Chile, Ecuador and Peru, at the First Conference on Exploitation and Conservation of the Maritime Resources of the South Pacific, held in Santiago de Chile in 1952, expressed their intention to sign Agreements or Conventions for the application of the principles related to that Sovereignty, in regarding the regulation and protection of hunting and fishing within the maritime zone that corresponds to it; AGREE: FIRST Chile, Ecuador and Peru will proceed by common agreement in the legal defense of the principle of sovereignty over the Maritime Zone up to a minimum distance of 200 nautical miles, including the respective soil and subsoil. It is understood that the nautical mile has an extension of one minute of arc on the Equator and that equals 1,852.8 meters. SECOND If either party receives claims or protests or they are formulated in against them claims before Courts of Law or Arbitration, general or special, the contracting countries undertake to consult each other on the bases of the defense and also undertake to provide the widest cooperation for a defense common. THIRD In the case of violation by means of fact of the maritime zone indicated, the State affected will give immediate notice to the other contracting parties to agree on the measures that agree to take to protect the affected sovereignty. FOURTH Each of the parties agrees not to enter into Agreements, Arrangements or Agreements that impair the sovereignty of the referred area, without detriment of their rights to enter into Agreements or enter into Contracts that are not contrary to the common standards established by the contracting countries. FIFTH Everything established in this Agreement shall be understood to be an integral part, supplementary and does not repeal the resolutions and agreements adopted in the Conference on the Exploitation and Conservation of the Maritime Resources of the South Pacific, held in Santiago de Chile, in August 1952.
